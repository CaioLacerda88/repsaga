One designer's hand

The body font needs to evolve from a safe default into a believable sibling that shares Rajdhani's DNA. Three candidates, rendered side-by-side against real RepSaga surfaces at production sizes.

1 · The diagnosis

The Rajdhani + Inter pairing is broken on four measurable axes, not on vibes:

  • X-height ratio. Rajdhani sits at ~68–70% of cap-height. Inter sits at ~75–78% — one of the highest in Google Fonts, by design (engineered for small-screen UI legibility). Result: in a list row where Inter title (16sp) follows a Rajdhani header (24sp), the Inter lowercase looks taller than expected, breaking the visual hierarchy the size numbers were meant to create.
  • Aperture philosophy. Rajdhani has tight, armored apertures — closed c, e, s; terminals cut at steep flat angles. Inter has the opposite — wide-mouthed c, fully open double-storey a, deliberately spacious. Adjacent, Inter reads slack against Rajdhani's blade-cut precision.
  • Weight ramp gap. Rajdhani ramp is 500 → 600 → 700 (three uniform steps). Inter ramp is 400 → 600 — skips 500 entirely. Inter 400 is optically lighter than Rajdhani 500, so body prose feels underpowered next to display.
  • Geometric vs humanist DNA. Rajdhani's R, K, G are constructed from geometric arcs and straight spurs — built, not drawn. Inter's g, y, f, j retain humanist pen-angle memory. Two different construction philosophies in the same scan path.
The fix isn't a "better humanist sans." A condensed-geometric display font pairs cleanly only with a body that shares its terminal style, monolinear stroke, and matched x-height — and ideally interlocks the weight ramp. Inter fails on all four. The candidates below pass on all four.

5 · Recommendation

Both research lenses (competitor scan + typographic-principle critique) independently ranked Barlow as #1. It is the only Google Fonts face that passes all four diagnosis axes simultaneously — terminal style, x-height, weight ramp, and DNA — without introducing new trade-offs that need surface-by-surface verification.

The pick — Barlow

  • Closest DNA match. California highway signage origin: condensed-to-normal width geometric sans, monolinear strokes, flat blade-cut terminals — nearly identical to Rajdhani's construction philosophy.
  • Bonus width axis. Barlow ships Condensed / Semi-Condensed / Regular from the same designer's hand. Barlow Condensed for the label/sectionHeader 11–12sp tracked uppercase slots picks up Rajdhani's verticality at micro-copy size, then Barlow Regular handles 14–16sp prose. Same font, two width axes — instantly reads as one design system.
  • Weight-ramp interlock. 400/500/600 body ramp plugs into Rajdhani 500/600/700 display ramp with zero perceptual gap.
  • No fitness-app entrenchment. Heavy use in esports/sports-graphics (ESPN score plates, several esports brand identities) but no major fitness app has claimed it. RepSaga gets the "engineered + competitive" register without inheriting a competitor's identity.

Runner-ups

  • DM Sans · safe. If post-iteration the team finds Barlow's industrial register too cold for the BR market, DM Sans is the cleanest fallback. It loses some personality but solves the dissonance and outperforms Barlow at 12sp textDim micro-copy.
  • Chakra Petch · bold. Strongest "same designer's hand" continuity with Rajdhani — highest RPG character of the three. The trade-off is real: paragraph prose at 14sp on narrow containers compresses noticeably. Viable only if form-tip / description copy is short (1–3 sentences), which it currently is.

What we explicitly considered and rejected

  • Exo 2 — PO's #2, but UX-critic's anti-rec. Looks like a match (geometric, sci-fi-adjacent, has Condensed cut), but x-height is too tall (~76%) and the lowercase a/g are subtly humanist. It would repeat Inter's size-hierarchy break.
  • Oswald — another condensed geometric, but its x-height (~63%) is too low and stroke contrast too high. At 14sp prose it degrades into a "word wall" — two condensed fonts in the same hierarchy without a width-axis differentiator.
  • Nunito Sans — rounded terminals + humanist warmth pull in the opposite direction from Rajdhani's cold geometric precision. The dissonance would be smaller than Inter but the tonal mismatch remains.

Implementation footprint (if Barlow wins)

  1. Bundle Barlow-Regular.ttf, Barlow-Medium.ttf, Barlow-SemiBold.ttf, Barlow-Bold.ttf, plus BarlowCondensed-SemiBold.ttf via pubspec.yaml > flutter.fonts (same loading contract as Rajdhani — direct TextStyle(fontFamily: ...), NOT the google_fonts async API).
  2. In lib/core/theme/app_theme.dart > AppTextStyles: swap fontFamily: 'Inter' → 'Barlow' for title, body, bodySmall. Swap 'Inter' → 'Barlow Condensed' for label, sectionHeader. Promote body weight 400 → 500 at textDim contexts (preserves perceived weight on dark surface).
  3. Update project_design_language_typography auto-memory: rule becomes "Rajdhani owns every number, every title, every label you read in a glance. Barlow (Condensed for micro-copy, Regular for prose) owns everything you read in a sentence."
  4. Update scripts/check_typography_call_sites.sh CI gate to also forbid fontFamily: 'Inter' outside app_theme.dart — same one-sanctioned-entry-point pattern.
  5. Visual-verification pass at 320 / 360 / 412dp on Saga, Home, Stats, Exercises, Routines, Workout-log, Profile-settings before merge (per CLAUDE.md visual-verification protocol).
