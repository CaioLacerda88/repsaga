Post-Phase-30 Design Exploration

Share Card Overlay + Post-Battle Summary

Two surfaces flagged in on-device verification after Phase 30 shipped. The share-card overlay lacks photo presence despite PR 30c typography bumps. The post-battle summary panel has dead real-estate after the cinematic ends. Eight proposals total, four per surface, each structurally distinct.

User (share card): Still feels too small / not present enough in the photo even after PR 30c bumped preview typography. Want more presence. Claim the moment.

User (summary panel): Not enough info, lot of free real estate. After cinematic crescendo, feels underwhelming.

Load-bearing constraints

  • Color tokens: --abyss #0D0319 · --hot-violet #B36DFF · --primary-violet #6A2FA8 · --hero-gold #FFB800 (PR/class-change ONLY) · body-part hues per BP. No raw hex outside AppColors.
  • Typography: Rajdhani 700 for numerics/display. Barlow Condensed 600 for tracked uppercase labels. Barlow for prose. No w800/w900.
  • Shape grammar: Hard edges. ClipPath diagonal cuts. Slash primitive (2dp, -8deg, at 28%). No border-radius on card chrome. No BoxShadow/blur. No glassmorphism.
  • Reward accent: heroGold reserved for PR moments and class-change only. Not routine XP or rank bars.
  • RPG thesis: Every element must pin that RPG progression is earned through real lifts, not check-ins or abstract stats.

Surface 1 — Share-Card Overlay

Sits on top of a user-taken photo at share preview. Current variants claim only the bottom strip or top+bottom diagonal collars. Each proposal below claims more photo real-estate. Photo background simulated as a CSS gym-equipment gradient.

Stamp / Sigil
  • Info hierarchy: Saga number at display size dominates the center. Class name second (identity). XP delta third (reward). Meta stats demoted to thin bottom strip.
  • Photo claim: The stamp sits at photo center, rotated -12°. The region behind it is darkened to ~70% opacity — still reads as gym context without destroying the photo. Everything outside the stamp block remains partially visible as atmosphere.
  • Angled rotation -12°: More severe than the -8° slash primitive. Intentional: stamps are applied physical objects, not UI overlays. The double border (outer 3dp + 5px inset 1dp) gives the stamp a die-cut quality without blur or shadow.
  • Slash relation: The 2dp violet slash crosses through the stamp body, below center mass. Reinforces RepSaga cut grammar. Not decorative: it anchors the overlay as a RepSaga artifact even without the wordmark.
  • Anti-generic: No rounded badge. No gradient pill. No glow. Stark rectangular double-border is typographic, not premium-UI.
  • Bottom strip: 10px Rajdhani wordmark left, Barlow Condensed meta right. Deliberately thin — the stamp is the hero, not the strip.
Borrows from: Persona 5R All-Out-Attack card / passport visa stamp / official rubber-stamp certificate aesthetics.
Risk: center placement may clash with photo subject. At very bright photos the desat-center region may still read as AI transparent overlay. Test at 1x pixel density on device.

Achievement Frame
  • Info hierarchy: The frame itself is the primary signal — the photo is presented as a trophy artifact. Top collar: Saga # + class name (identity). Bottom collar: XP delta (reward) + compact meta + wordmark. Photo occupies 55-60% of card area.
  • Photo claim: Diagonal-clipped collars cut INTO the photo with angular geometry. The 4dp accent bars complete the frame on left and right edges. Together they claim all four photo edges without obscuring center content.

Borrows from: Championship sports trading cards · polaroid with heavy matte border · Hermes agenda insert tabs.
Risk: at very light photos the collar edges may lose contrast. A 1dp inner stroke on the clip edges resolves this. Keep that in scope for implementation.

Big-Stat Overlay
  • Info hierarchy: The XP number IS the entire design. At 80sp Rajdhani 700 it occupies approximately 30% of the card height. Nothing else competes. The eyebrow (8.5sp) and unit label (14sp) serve as rulers for the number, not as equal-weight elements.

Overlay — Side-by-Side Comparison

D1 — Stamp
Angled center-stamp with double border, class name, XP. Photo visible as field. Stamp is a physical applied object, not a UI overlay.
Tradeoff: center placement may clash with photo subject. Needs smart crop guidance or photo-region detection.
D2 — Scoreboard
Header + photo-mid + stat-footer. Locker-room fight-card aesthetic. Stats in a three-column grid with hard vertical rules.
Tradeoff: works best when photo subject lives in the center mid-zone. Thin subjects or landscape crops may leave awkward dead zones.
Top Pick
D3 — Frame
Diagonal ClipPath collars + 4dp BP-hue side bars. The body-part dominant color changes every session, encoding data into chrome structure. Works on any photo composition.
Tradeoff: collar edges may lose contrast on very light photos. Needs a 1dp inner stroke safety net. Implementation requires ClipPath precision at pixel density.
D4 — Big Stat
XP number at 30% card height IS the design. Photo darkened as field. Slashes cut through. Maximum bold claim. Data-as-identity.
Tradeoff: only works when XP number is impressive. Low-XP sessions may feel self-defeating to share. Needs minimum-threshold logic before offering this template.

My pick: Direction 3 — Achievement Frame. It is the only proposal that encodes session data (dominant body-part hue) into the card chrome itself. The frame changes color every session without redesigning anything. The ClipPath collars solve the presence problem by claiming all four edges simultaneously. It works regardless of photo subject composition — the photo is always the trophy inside the frame.

Surface 2 — Post-Battle Summary Panel

After the cinematic ends, the summary panel mounts. Current state: Saga line + meta + NEXT eyebrow + two CTAs + large empty void. These proposals fill that void with RPG-grounded, lift-level data. All four proposals share the same header and CTA structure; only the information body differs.

Available data: per-body-part XP delta · session PRs · closest rank-up callout · class progress near threshold · title progress (1-2 sessions away). Not available: streak/consistency · comparison to last session.

Direction 1
Rank Rail
  • Info hierarchy: XP total (primary) · six body-part rank bars (secondary, all visible without scrolling) · closest rank-up callout (tertiary) · CTAs pinned to bottom.
  • Data density without scroll: Six 6dp bars at equal spacing fit within the panel. Each bar encodes: color (which BP), fill (current rank position in tier), a white tick (this session's XP delta placement), current rank number, and a +LVL badge when a threshold was crossed.
  • Level-up moment: The +LVL badge is the celebration signal for the panel. In implementation this would be a 300ms opacity pulse on the bar fill during panel mount. Single animation, state-change only, no loop.
  • RPG thesis: Every bar maps to a real body part worked in real lifts. The bar only fills when you train that muscle. You cannot game it by checking in.
  • Anti-generic: No progress ring. No donut chart. Flat bars, hard edges, monochromatic per body-part hue token. The bar IS the data.
  • Empty-space resolution: The 6-bar rail fills the full panel height between XP header and CTAs. No dead space.
Borrows from: Pokemon EV/IV stat bars · fighting game per-attribute character radar stripped to bars.
Risk: sessions training only 1-2 BPs show 4-5 nearly empty bars. Recommend showing all 6 always — the neglected bars are information, not failure states. Must frame the eyebrow label accordingly.

Card Stack
  • Info hierarchy: Active (front) card gets full attention — one fact, maximum. Hidden cards are partially visible behind at slight rotation, teasing content. Tap or swipe left to cycle.
  • Card order = narrative arc: Front = biggest win (PR if exists, otherwise dominant BP rank-up). Card 2 = next immediate target. Card 3 = session dominant BP XP summary. Card 4 = title progress. The sequence is dramatic: most exciting first, planning horizon last.
  • Dual engagement mode: Users who want to exit fast ignore the stack and tap CONTINUE. Users who want to marinate swipe through 4 cards. The design respects both modes without padding the default view.
  • heroGold PR card: The PR card uses heroGold eyebrow label ONLY when a PR exists. No PR this session = no gold anywhere. Scarcity preserved.
  • ClipPath corner notch: Each card uses polygon clip with a notched bottom-right corner — hard edge, no border-radius. Physical card-deck presence without any shadow or blur.

Risk: if user has no PR and no rank-up, the front card defaults to a bland stat. Need a fallback card hierarchy spec. Also: horizontal swipe may conflict with parent screen vertical scroll on Android — wrap in a GestureDetector with direction locking.

  • Label column as structure: 38dp label column (Barlow Condensed 600 tracked uppercase) gives the layout a spec-sheet quality. Labels and values are instantly distinguishable at a glance: same font, different role (label=dim+tracked, value=cream+Rajdhani 700).

Design Axioms for Implementation Handoff

These constraints apply to whichever proposals advance to implementation. Non-negotiable.

  • No border-radius on card chrome. Cards, strips, collars: hard rectangular or ClipPath polygon. The only exception is the internal corner notch (polygon clip) which reads as a cut, not a curve.
  • heroGold is scarcity. One instance per screen, on the highest-value reward signal. In the summary panel: only on the PR row value or PR card eyebrow. In the share card: only on the PR flag in the bottom strip. No PR = no gold anywhere on that surface.
  • Slash primitive is structural, not decorative. It appears once per surface. In the share card it cuts through the overlay and anchors the card as a RepSaga artifact. In the summary it separates header from content body. Multiple slashes used as wallpaper = rejected.
  • Font splits are non-negotiable. Rajdhani 700 for every number, every saga/rank label, every XP value. Barlow Condensed 600 for tracked uppercase section labels. Barlow for narration prose and lift names. Cross-mixing degrades scannability in the 2-second between-set window.
  • Body-part hues are identity tokens. Arms #F87171 · Back #38BDF8 · Chest #F472B6 · Legs #4ADE80 · Shoulders #FBBF24 · Core #A78BFA. Never use primaryViolet or hotViolet as a body-part color. Never use a BP hue as a brand UI accent.
  • Motion budget. Panel mount: slide up 280ms ease-out. Bar fills: staggered 50ms per rail, 220ms each. Level-up badge: single 300ms opacity pulse on mount. PR card entrance: 200ms scale 0.96 to 1.0. No looping animations. No entrance choreography for aesthetic purposes only.
  • Dominant body-part derivation. For any surface that uses the dominant BP (share card left bar, debrief header, journal LED BY row): dominant BP = BP that contributed the most XP this session. Tie-break by volume. Must be computed server-side or in the state machine choreographer, not in the widget layer.
